Hong Kong Trail

12 Feb, 2022
[Blog No. 1]
  • Route: HKU -> The Peak -> Big Wave Bay
  • Length: 47.3 km
  • Elevation: 1,865 m
  • Difficulty: 5*
  • Time: 13:19 with 11:55 moving time

The desire to do Hong Kong Trail in one go came very strongly after the challenge of "劏青蛙" ----- a 20-ish km hiking trail across HK Island from the western to the eastern point, which was the longest hike I have ever done at that time.

After carefully going through the infomation about all 8 stages of HK Trail, I decided to have a try anyways. The most difficulty one among all sections is the Jardine's Lookout, with an elevation of more than 400m with long stairs, which I have done many times and reckoned it a not-so-difficult one. Ironically, it drained me inside out this time.

The starting point is aounrd the Peak Galleria. Since we had breakfast at Kenney Town, we decided to hike up to The Peak via The University of Hong Kong (HKU), which led us stright to distance post H005.

The first two sections were very easy, with mostly flatten concrete trails. Even though the temperature rose as the sun came out, it was not very hot since there were lots of trees and streams along. We reached Peel Rise within two hours, ready to take on Stage 3.
